# Stop Ignoring Your Health Insurance: The Rs 20,000 Tax Secret

In Nepal, most people think about insurance only when they buy a new bike or car. We happily pay thousands of rupees for motor insurance because it is mandatory by law. But when it comes to our own bodies, we hesitate.

Medical emergencies are the number one cause of sudden financial ruin for middle-class families in Kathmandu. A single week in a private hospital ICU can easily wipe out five years of your hard-earned savings.

Currently, the vast majority of healthcare expenses in Nepal are paid "out-of-pocket." This means when tragedy strikes, families are forced to empty their bank accounts, sell off land, or take high-interest loans from relatives just to cover hospital bills.

## The Rs 20,000 Income Tax Hack
Here is the best part that most agents fail to mention: The Nepal Income Tax Act actively encourages you to protect your health.

If you buy a medical insurance policy from a resident insurer, you are entitled to deduct up to **Rs 20,000** from your gross taxable income every single year.

* **How it works:** If you are a salaried professional paying a 20% or 30% income tax rate, deducting Rs 20,000 from your taxable income effectively means the government is paying for a significant chunk of your health insurance premium!
* You get the financial shield of hospital coverage, *and* your TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) goes down. It is a mathematical win-win.
