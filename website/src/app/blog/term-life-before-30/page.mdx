## The Difference Between Health Insurance and Term Life
Before we go further, let's clear up the jargon.
* **Health Insurance** (what your office provides) pays for medical expenses while you are alive.
* **Term Life Insurance** is a pure protection plan. If something happens to you, it pays a massive lump sum to your family.
* **Endowment Plans** (what your local agent/uncle probably tried to sell you) are "savings" plans with high premiums and low life cover.

For a young professional, Term Life is the gold standard because it offers the highest "Sum Assured" (payout) for the lowest possible premium.

## Why the "Age 30" Deadline Matters
In the eyes of the 14 Life Insurance companies in Nepal, your 20s are your "Golden Window."

* **Locked-in Low Premiums:** If you buy a Term Life policy at 25, your annual premium stays the same for the next 30 years. If you wait until you are 35 or 40, you could end up paying double or triple for the exact same coverage.
* **No Medical Stress:** At 28, you are likely healthy. At 38, you might have blood pressure or cholesterol issues that make insurance companies reject your application or charge you a "loading" fee.
