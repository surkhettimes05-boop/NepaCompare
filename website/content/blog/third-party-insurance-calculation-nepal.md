In Nepal, having Third-Party Motor Insurance is legally mandatory under the Motor Vehicles and Transport Management Act. Whether you drive an old 150cc bike or a brand new SUV, you cannot renew your Bluebook without it.

But how exactly is the premium calculated, and why is it non-negotiable?

## What is Third-Party Insurance?

Third-party insurance **does not** cover damages to your own vehicle. 
If you cause an accident, this insurance covers:
1.  **Death or bodily injury** to a third party (pedestrian or passenger in another vehicle).
2.  **Property damage** to a third party's vehicle or property.

Because this is a mandatory social protection mechanism, the premiums are strictly regulated by the **Beema Samiti (Insurance Board of Nepal)**.

## How the Premium is Calculated

Unlike Comprehensive Insurance (which depends on your vehicle's market value or IDV), Third-Party Insurance premiums are fixed based on two main factors:
1.  **Engine Capacity (CC) / Kw for EVs**
2.  **Vehicle Type and Use** (Private vs. Commercial)

Every insurance company in Nepal (Shikhar, Sagarmatha, NLG, etc.) must charge the **exact same base premium** for third-party insurance.

### 1. Two-Wheelers (Bikes & Scooters)
For private two-wheelers, the annual premium is categorized by engine displacement:
*   **Up to 150 cc:** NPR 1,500 (approx)
*   **151 cc to 250 cc:** NPR 1,700 (approx)
*   **251 cc and above:** NPR 1,900 (approx)

*(Note: These are base indicative rates and are subject to 13% VAT and stamp duty).*

### 2. Private Cars (Four-Wheelers)
For private cars, the base premium is significantly higher and also scales with CC:
*   **Up to 1000 cc:** NPR 3,500 (approx)
*   **1001 cc to 1600 cc:** NPR 4,500 (approx)
*   **1601 cc to 2000 cc:** NPR 5,500 (approx)
*   **2001 cc and above:** NPR 6,500+ (approx)

### Electric Vehicles (EVs)
EVs are calculated based on Kilowatts (Kw). The Beema Samiti often provides a discount on the premium for EVs to promote green energy. For example, a standard electric scooter might have a lower third-party premium compared to a petrol scooter of equivalent power.

## Adding Passenger Cover

Your base third-party premium covers external parties. However, you must also buy mandatory cover for the driver and pillion rider/passengers. 
*   **Accidental Death/Disability Cover:** Typically, this costs an additional NPR 500 per passenger, providing a cover of up to NPR 5 Lakhs per person.

## The Final Calculation
Your final receipt will look something like this:
**Base Premium (by CC) + Passenger/Driver Cover + Stamp Duty (NPR 50) + 13% VAT = Total Payable.**

Because these rates are fixed by the government, if an agent is offering you a "discount" on third-party insurance, they are likely selling a fake policy or taking the discount out of their own commission.
