Filing an insurance claim for your bike or scooter in Nepal can feel overwhelming, especially after the stress of an accident. However, if you understand the process and have the right documents, getting your settlement is actually quite straightforward.

Whether you have Third-Party Liability or a Comprehensive (Full Cover) policy, this step-by-step guide will walk you through exactly what you need to do to claim your two-wheeler insurance in Nepal.

## Step 1: Immediate Actions Post-Accident

Before you think about insurance, ensure everyone's safety.
1. **Check for injuries:** If anyone is hurt, call an ambulance or go to the nearest hospital immediately.
2. **Do not move the vehicles:** Unless they are causing a massive traffic hazard, leave the vehicles exactly where they are. This is crucial for the police report.
3. **Call the Traffic Police (103):** You must report the incident to the traffic police immediately. 

## Step 2: File the FIR (First Information Report)

Insurance companies in Nepal will **not** process a major claim without an FIR. 
Go to the nearest police station (where the accident occurred) and file an FIR. You will need a copy of this report to submit to your insurer.

## Step 3: Inform Your Insurance Company

You must notify your insurance company within **3 to 7 days** (depending on the insurer's policy terms, but the sooner, the better).
*   Call their toll-free number or customer support.
*   Provide your Policy Number and a brief description of the accident.
*   They will assign a **Surveyor** to evaluate the damage.

> **IMPORTANT:** Do NOT repair your bike before the surveyor inspects it. If you fix it before the inspection, the insurance company will likely reject your claim.

## Step 4: The Surveyor's Inspection

The surveyor will visit the police station or the garage where your bike is kept. They will take photos, assess the damage, and verify the details against your FIR and policy document.
Ensure you are present during this inspection to point out all the damages.

## Step 5: Submit the Required Documents

To process the claim, you must submit a physical or digital copy of the following documents to the insurance company:
*   Duly filled and signed Claim Form (provided by the insurer).
*   Copy of your Bluebook (Vehicle Registration).
*   Copy of your valid Driving License.
*   Copy of your Insurance Policy.
*   Original Police Report / FIR.
*   Estimated repair bill from a registered garage.

## Step 6: Repair and Reimbursement

Once the surveyor approves the estimated cost, you can proceed with the repairs. 
*   **Cashless Garages:** If you repair at a garage tied up with your insurer, the insurance company will pay the garage directly (minus your compulsory deductible/excess and depreciation).
*   **Reimbursement:** If you use a non-network garage, you must pay the bill first and submit the original tax invoice to the insurer for reimbursement.

### Understanding Depreciation and Deductibles

You will rarely get 100% of the repair cost back. 
*   **Compulsory Deductible (Excess):** A fixed amount (usually NPR 1,000 to 2,000) you must pay out of pocket before the insurer pays the rest.
*   **Depreciation:** Parts like plastic, rubber, and glass have a high depreciation rate. The insurer will only pay a percentage of the cost of these new parts based on the age of your bike.
