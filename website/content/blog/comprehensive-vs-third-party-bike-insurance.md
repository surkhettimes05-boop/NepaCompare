With over 2 million registered two-wheelers in Nepal, the roads are crowded, and accidents happen. When renewing your bike or scooter insurance, you are faced with a choice: Should you get the mandatory **Third-Party Liability** cover, or upgrade to a **Comprehensive Policy**?

Let’s break it down.

## What is Third-Party Insurance?

Third-party insurance is legally mandatory in Nepal. If you are caught riding without it, the traffic police will issue a fine.
*   **What it covers:** It pays for injuries, death, or property damage you cause to *someone else* (the third party) in an accident.
*   **What it does NOT cover:** It pays absolutely nothing for damages to your own bike or your own medical bills.

## What is Comprehensive Insurance?

As the name suggests, this covers much more.
*   **Own Damage:** It covers repair costs if your bike is damaged in an accident, natural disaster (floods/landslides), or fire.
*   **Theft:** If your bike is stolen, comprehensive insurance reimburses you based on the Insured Declared Value (IDV) of your vehicle.
*   **Third-Party:** It also includes the mandatory third-party liability cover.

## Which One Should You Choose?

If you have a brand-new bike or a high-end model (like a Royal Enfield or Duke), **Comprehensive Insurance is highly recommended**. The cost of replacing parts is too high to risk.

If you are riding a 10-year-old scooter where the market value is extremely low, a simple Third-Party policy might suffice to keep you street-legal.
